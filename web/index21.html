About the Simulation Playground

Build Real Workflows

What happens when a Monaco-powered JSON editor meets drag-and-drop timeline manipulation? A system that works how you want to work. Design processes with actors, equipment, and resources that interact like they would in the real world, or define your own, custom objects. There's no need for abstract boxes and arrows - actually see what's happening at a given time.

See What Breaks Immediately

Our validation engine runs a growing library of computational checks as you design. Resource conflicts, timing violations, structural integrity, scheduling overlaps - all caught in real-time. Your simulation gets validated against profitability metrics, resource utilisation, equipment capacity constraints, and more before you finish building it.

Custom Metrics for Validation

Got your own metrics? Define them in simple JavaScript in the powerful Metrics Editor. This infuses the power of the simulation playground with your own logic, enabling precise validation of all your simulations. Custom metrics ensure your simulations reflect exactly what matters most to you.

Physical. Digital. Displays.

Simulation Playground has got support for physical and digital locations, as well as digital objects and a system for defining UI elements of displays. How complex is that backup workflow? How many flights of stairs does that delivery driver need to climb? How are the orders for my website handled? Answer all these questions and more with the simulation playground.

How The Engine Actually Works

Timeline-Based Modelling

Visual drag-and-drop editor for task scheduling, resource allocation, and actor coordination. Move tasks between actors, resize durations, add dependencies—all whilst the validation engine runs computational checks in the background.

Real-Time Metrics Computation

The validation system automatically computes throughput rates, resource efficiency percentages, labour utilisation, cost per unit, cycle times, and error rates. Instant feedback on whether your process makes economic sense, and ensures that it complies with the laws of physics.

Universal Object Model

Standardised simulation schema that distinguishes between persistent equipment (with states like clean/dirty/in-use) and consumable resources (with quantities that get depleted). Tasks can interact with objects by modifying their properties.

Interactions Made Simple

Tasks can make use of the 'interactions' concept. The simulation engine will execute the interactions defined in tasks when the task start time is reached. This means that tasks can modify objects in any way - from changing its emoji, to reducing the capacity of a server during a hardware upgrade.

Predict your revenue

You can use the simulation playground to model your business' processes, and see the timescale over a longer period of time. This empowers you to see exactly how much revenue you are expected to receive over the course of months, or even years.

Why This Matters

Most automation projects fail not because the technology doesn't work, but because the process design doesn't account for real-world constraints. Resource conflicts, timing dependencies, equipment limitations, and human factors. These are predictable problems that a simulation would catch early.

By testing automation workflows in a validated simulation environment, you can identify genuine bottlenecks, understand real resource requirements, and make informed decisions about what's actually worth automating.

Get Started

Jump into the Simulation Playground and start building. There's a getting started tutorial under the 'Help' menu to help you get to grips with the interface. Build something, watch it run, see what breaks, fix it, repeat.

The community wiki grows as more people contribute working simulations and insights. But even if you never share anything, you'll understand automation better by building one validated simulation than by reading a dozen theoretical papers.
